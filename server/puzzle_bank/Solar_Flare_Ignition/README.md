# Solar Flare Ignition

**Objective**: Determine the peak radiation frequency of the ignition sequence.

Deep within the solar core, an ignition sequence has been detected. To calibrate our sensors, we must calculate the peak wavelength ($\lambda_{max}$) of the flare's radiation using Wien's Displacement Law:

$$\lambda_{max} = \frac{b}{T}$$

Where:
- $b$ (Wien's Constant) $\approx 2.898 \times 10^6\text{ nm}\cdot\text{ K}$
- $T$ (Temperature) is the temperature of the flare.

**The Task**:
Our thermal sensors have clocked the flare at exactly **10,000,000 K** ($10^7\text{ K}$).

Find the peak wavelength in **picometers (pm)**. 
*(Note: 1 nm = 1,000 pm)*

Enter the numerical value only.
